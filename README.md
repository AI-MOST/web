# AI-MOST Webpage

<h2 id="ai-most-webpage">AI-MOST = AI, Metaheuristics, and Optimization for Science and Technology </h1>

<p>Xin-She Yang is <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reader_(academic_rank)">Reader</a> in Modelling and Simulation as well as Optimization at Middlesex University London, with more than 25 years’ experience in teaching and research. He got his DPhil in Applied Mathematics from Mathematical Institute, University of Oxford in 1998. He then worked at Cambridge University Engineering Department and later as a Senior Research Scientist at Mathematics and Scientific Computing Division of UK’s National Physical Laboratory.</p>

<p>He is also an elected Fellow of the <em>Institute of Mathematics and its Applications</em> (2021), Fellow of <em>Asian Computational Intelligence Society</em> (2023) and Fellow of <em>Soft Computing Research Society</em> (2025). He is the co-Editor for Springer’s book series: <a href="https://www.springer.com/series/16134">Springer Tracts in Nature-Inspired Computing</a> and has been on  editorial boards of multiple international journals, such as <em>Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence</em> (Elsevier) and <em>Journal of Computational Science</em> (Elsevier). He is also a member of the UKRI Talent Peer Review College.</p>

<p>He has authored/edited more than 50 books and has published more than 400 peer-reviewed research papers with more than 96,000 citations, and the full lists of his publications are available at <a href="https://scholar.google.co.uk/citations?user=fA6aTlAAAAAJ">Google scholar</a> and at <a href="https://www.webofscience.com/wos/author/record/I-5662-2019">Web of Science</a>. He has been on the prestigious list of most influential researchers or <a href="https://www.webofscience.com/wos/author/record/I-5662-2019">highly-cited researchers</a>  (i.e., top 0.1% Scientists, Web of Science) for ten consecutive years since 2016.</p>
